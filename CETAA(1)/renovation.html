A Campaign by CETAA

Restore the hall that has witnessed our jubilees.

Diamond Jubilee Hall has hosted CET's biggest institutional moments for decades. We're raising funds to renovate it — new air-conditioning and upgraded ancillary spaces — so it's ready for the next fifty years.

Contribute now See the plan
Why This Hall

Every major chapter of CET's recent history was written here.

In September 2015, Kerala's Chief Minister stood in Diamond Jubilee Hall to inaugurate the ECE department's Golden Jubilee year and lay the foundation stone for its new research block. It's the room CET reaches for whenever something is worth gathering the whole college together for — convocations, jubilees, ministerial visits, and the everyday seminars that shape a student's four years.

Decades of heavy use have caught up with it. The cooling system struggles through Trivandrum's heat, and the spaces around the main hall — green rooms, storage, access points — were never built for the scale of events it now hosts.

Project Overview

What your contribution renovates.

The scope is deliberately focused: comfort, accessibility and dignity for everyone who walks into the hall — not a cosmetic makeover.

01

Full air-conditioning installation

Replacing the hall's outdated cooling with a properly sized HVAC system, so packed convocations no longer compete with Trivandrum's heat.

02

Green rooms & backstage spaces

Dedicated, weatherproofed rooms for speakers, performers and organisers — currently a recurring scramble before every event.

03

Accessibility upgrades

Ramps, signage and seating adjustments so alumni, faculty and guests of every age and ability can attend comfortably.

04

Lighting, acoustics & finishing

Updated stage lighting and sound treatment befitting a hall that regularly hosts ministers, scientists and visiting dignitaries.

The Goal

Funded entirely by alumni, for alumni.

CETAA has self-funded every major campus contribution to date — 250 laptops during the pandemic, scholarships for over a thousand students, and now, this. No contribution is too small.
