The Legacy

A college older than independent India's engineering ambitions.

CET was established by royal charter in Travancore — and its alumni have spent half a century formalising that bond into an association nearly as storied as the college itself.

1939

The first classes begin

Under Maharajah Sree Chithira Thirunal Balarama Varma, College of Engineering Trivandrum opens as the first technical institution in the Travancore state — starting in the Chief Engineer's own bungalow.

1960

A campus of its own

CET moves to its present 125-acre campus, giving generations of CETians the grounds, hostels and laboratories they still return to.

1976

CETAA is formed

Following early conversations led by Dr. C. P. Kuriakose and a core group of alumni, the College of Engineering Trivandrum Alumni Association is formally established to keep CETians connected to their alma mater.

2015

Diamond Jubilee Hall takes centre stage

The hall hosts the Golden Jubilee celebrations of the ECE department, inaugurated by Kerala's Chief Minister — one of many institutional milestones held within its walls.

2023

Campus hours extended

CET becomes the first state-run college in Kerala to keep its libraries and labs open until 9pm, nearly doubling research access for students — a change alumni had long pushed for.

Giving Back

Every CETian who needed a hand, found one.

CETAA's scholarship schemes exist for one reason: no student should leave CET because they couldn't afford to stay.

☉

GEMS

Growing Engineers' Monetary Support — direct financial assistance for students facing hardship, funded entirely by alumni contributions.

☉

CARE

Continuous Assistance for Rising Engineers — sustained, multi-year support so a single setback never ends a CET education.

☉

HOPE Plus

Helping Outstanding Pupils in Education — run with CETA Galaxy Charitable Trust, supporting over a thousand meritorious students across Kerala.
